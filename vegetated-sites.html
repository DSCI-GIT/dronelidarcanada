Bare-Earth LiDAR for Vegetated and Forested Sites

When the ground is hidden by trees, brush, or uneven cover, drone LiDAR can collect returns that support bare-earth classification and terrain modelling.

Request a LiDAR Quote
Why LiDAR

Imagery sees the top. LiDAR helps model the ground.

Photogrammetry depends on visible surface texture. In vegetated areas, that often means the canopy, not the ground. Drone LiDAR can capture multiple returns and support classification workflows that separate ground from vegetation.

For engineers and developers, the useful result is not a flashy point cloud. It is a bare-earth elevation model that helps reveal slopes, drainage paths, access constraints, and grade conditions.

Good fit

Where bare-earth LiDAR is most useful.

Forested development land

Support early-stage terrain review before clearing, detailed field visits, or design work.

Rural and drainage sites

Understand elevation, slopes, swales, and low areas across large, uneven properties.

Corridors and access routes

Evaluate long, vegetated, or difficult-to-access routes with efficient aerial coverage.

Outputs

From canopy returns to ground deliverables.

Vegetated projects are scoped around the data your team needs, not just the flight. Deliverables may include classified point clouds, bare-earth DTM/DEM, contours, CAD-ready surfaces, imagery, and reports.

  • Ground/non-ground classification where required
  • Bare-earth models for engineering and planning review
  • Survey-grade workflows with control and checkpoints where required
